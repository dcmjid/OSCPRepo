You can work with strings/grep/xxd and other utilities directly on a disk!
strings /dev/sdb #recovers things that may still be readable (including deleted) files

Otherwise, use other forensic tools

grep for a flag:
grep -a '[a-z0-9]\{32\}' /dev/sdb #search for a string 32 characters long with alpha lower and 0-9
  # -a flag to treat binary blobs as ASCII

#can also use -B2 and -A2 in order to also grep BEFORE 2 lines and AFTER 2 lines

grep -a B2 -A5 'off the USB' /dev/sdb


Remote copy a drive
ssh pi@<IP> "sudo dcfldd if=/dev/sdb | gzip -1 -" | dcfldd of=pi.dd.gz #gzip on the fly, to stdout and pipe to local dclfdd of

gunzip -d to unzip our image

binwalk -Me to extract things #may or may not find everything (ie text files)
photorec / test disk to recover things from our image


Sometimes forensic tools miss things and manually examining can be fruitful
