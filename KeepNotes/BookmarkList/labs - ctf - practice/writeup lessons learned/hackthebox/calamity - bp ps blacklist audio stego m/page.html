LXD is the Linux container hypervisor. Good writeup of privesc here 

Being a member of any hypervisor group could be a way to privesc (whether its qemu, vmware, docker, lxd, etc)
In the case of LXD:

lxc init ubuntu:1604 blah -c security.privileged=true
lxc config device add blah root disk source=/ path=/.nt/root recursive=true

Essentially, the lxc group has the ability to mount the harddrive to the container and view it's files regardless of security permissions (assuming because of setting security.privileged=true)

LXD post install adds any current members of the "sudo" group also to the "lxd" group
